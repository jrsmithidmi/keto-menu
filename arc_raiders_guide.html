üõ°Ô∏è Understanding Augments

Augments are the core equipment system in Arc Raiders that determine what shields you can carry and provide special perks to enhance your survival.

Shield Compatibility

The primary difference between augments is their shield compatibility:

Light Shields

Entry-level protection. Compatible with basic augments like Looting MK1.

Medium Shields

Balanced protection. Requires better augments to equip.

Heavy Shields

Maximum protection. Only compatible with top-tier augments like Combat Augments.

Augment Types & Perks

Combat Augment: Restores 1 health every 5 seconds when damage is taken (pauses for 30 seconds after activation). Compatible with all shield types (light, medium, heavy).

Looting Series

  • Looting MK1: Light shields only, basic starting augment
  • Looting MK2: 3 extra trinket slots - perfect for money runs
  • Looting MK3 Survivor: 3 extra healing item slots - best for survivability
Pro Tip: Choose your augment based on your mission type. Going for loot? Use MK2. Need to survive tough encounters? MK3 Survivor is your friend.

üéí Inventory Management Essentials

Priority #1: Expand your stash as many times as possible, as fast as possible. This is the single most important upgrade you can make.

What to Extract (Priority Order)

üî´ Weapons - HIGHEST PRIORITY

Weapons are one of the best things you can extract because:

  • You can use them immediately to progress easier
  • Recycling them gives valuable materials (advanced mechanical components, heavy gun parts, etc.)
  • They provide modification materials based on attachments
Example: A level 3 Volcano recycled gives advanced mechanical components and heavy gun parts - both critical for upgrades.

üõ°Ô∏è Shields - HIGH PRIORITY

  • Purple Shields: Always extract
  • Blue Shields: Extract even if damaged - they recycle into Arc Circuitry for repairs
  • Green Shields: Leave behind

‚öôÔ∏è Modifications

  • Green Mods: Extract at the beginning of the game
  • White mods can be crafted easily at workbenches
  • Legendary Gear: ALWAYS extract no matter what

üîß Combat Augments (Blue)

While useful, these are not hard to craft at workbenches. Pick them up if convenient, but don't drop strong weapons for them.

üí∞ Scrappy's Passive Income System

Scrappy is one of the most important NPCs in the game. Here's why:

How Scrappy Works:
  1. Feed Scrappy fruits (don't delete them!)
  2. Scrappy brings free materials over time
  3. As you upgrade Scrappy, he brings more and better materials
  4. Excess materials = free money

Materials Scrappy Provides:

  • Metal Parts
  • Fabric
  • Plastic Parts
  • Assorted Seeds (very important - see Traders section)
Example: Excess plastic parts can be sold for 10,000+ credits. You can easily make 20k in seconds by selling Scrappy's surplus materials!

üîã Arc Power Cells - Dual Purpose

These cells from Arc enemies serve two functions:

  • In Battle: Use immediately for 2 shield bars
  • Crafting: Combine them to create Shield Charger (4 bars - fully recharges green shields)

üî® Workbench Priority Guide

Not all workbenches are created equal. Here's the optimal upgrade order:

ü•á #1 Priority: The Refiner

This is a no-brainer upgrade! The Refiner lets you craft all the critical components you need.

Refiner Capabilities by Level:

  • Level 1: Craft basic Mechanical Components
  • Level 2: Craft Advanced Components (mechanical & electrical)
  • Level 3:
    • Arc Circuitries (repair shields)
    • Power Rods (create purple shields)
Why It's #1: You can mass-craft components that would otherwise require hours of farming. Just throw in 10 and craft them instantly!

ü•à #2 Priority: Gear Bench

Directly tied to your augments - essential for different playstyles.

What You Can Craft:

  • Combat Augments (different shield compatibilities)
  • Looting Augments (MK1, MK2, MK3)
  • Blue Shields: 1 Arc Circuitry + Batteries
  • Purple Shields: Higher-level crafting
Flexibility: Craft augments for money runs, survival runs, or tactical missions on demand.

ü•â #3 Priority: Gunsmith

Gunsmith Capabilities:

  • Level 2: Craft awesome weapons
  • Level 3: Unlock blueprint crafting (Betatina, Jupiter, etc.)

Lower Priority Workbenches

Medical Lab

Normal shield rechargers and herb bandages work well enough early game. Upgrade this later when you need advanced healing options.

Utility & Explosive Station

Situational upgrades. Focus on the top 3 first, then work on these based on your playstyle.

üè™ Traders - Daily Resets & Must-Buys

üå± Celeste - The Free Materials Trader

Why Scrappy + Celeste = Profit: Scrappy gives you free Assorted Seeds ‚Üí Trade seeds at Celeste for valuable materials. It's essentially free crafting supplies!

What to Buy Daily from Celeste:

  • Batteries - Critical for shield crafting (10 daily)
  • Steel Springs - Needed for attachments
  • Simple Gun Parts - Only if low on stock
  • Duct Tape - Essential for attachments (runs out quickly)
  • Voltage Converters - Purple shield crafting
Pro Tip: Check Celeste EVERY DAY and buy as much as you can with Assorted Seeds. Don't waste the daily reset limits!

üî´ Gun Shop - Tanwen (Daily Reset)

Recommended Purchases:

  • Hullcracker - 30,000 credits
    • Buy almost every reset
    • Extremely powerful against Arc enemies (PvE)
    • Stock up - you can never have too many
  • Renegade - Optional if you're a fan
Avoid: Green weapons are overpriced. You'll find plenty naturally while playing. Most blue weapons can also be skipped.
Note: 30,000 credits seems like a lot at first, but it's very manageable once you understand the game economy. The Hullcracker is worth it!

üè• The Clinic (Daily Reset)

Smart Daily Purchases:

  • Blue Shields - 18,000 credits
    • Daily reset - buy consistently
    • Build up inventory for quick equips
  • Purple Shields
    • More expensive but worth stocking
    • Having shields ready saves time
  • Shield Rechargers - Only when running low
Strategy: Even if you don't need shields immediately, buying them on reset and stockpiling means you'll always have shields ready when you need them.

üèÜ Trials & Projects Explained

‚öîÔ∏è Trials - Weekly Achievement System

Trials is a competitive achievement system with leaderboards that resets weekly.

How It Works:
  1. Complete weekly tasks for points
  2. Higher points = better rank
  3. Aim for scores beyond the minimum for faster progression
  4. MUST EXTRACT after completing tasks or points don't count!

Rank Progression

Rank Description
Rookie Starting rank
Try Hard Second tier
Wildcard Mid tier
Daredevil High tier
Hot Shot Elite tier
Cantina Legend Top 100 players globally
Critical Rule: Even if you score 13,000 points when only 4,000 is required, it doesn't matter if you DON'T EXTRACT!

Example Weekly Tasks

  • Search First Wave Husks - 4,000 points (score higher for better ranking)
  • Destroy Ticks, Fireballs, and Pops - 4,000 points
  • Tasks change weekly - stay flexible

üîÑ Projects - Account Reset System

Projects allow you to completely reset your character for long-term bonuses.

How Projects Work:

  • Complete 6 stages of challenges
  • After completion, you can wipe your character
  • Lose most progress BUT gain permanent bonuses

What You LOSE on Reset

Lost Items
Stash upgrade progress
Inventory items
Blueprints
Coins
Player level
Skill points
Raider Den progress
Workshop quests

What You KEEP on Reset

  • Cosmetics
  • Account progression
  • Other permanent unlocks

üéÅ Reset Bonuses - Why It's Worth It

Permanent Bonuses Per Reset:
  • Bonus skill points earned from expeditions
  • Bonus stash slots earned from expeditions
  • Exclusive cosmetics
Long-Term Strategy: After 3, 5, or 10 resets, your account becomes significantly stronger with bonus skill points and stash slots. You're essentially getting paid to reset in a meaningful way!
Recommended: Reset your account every time you complete the 6 project stages. The permanent bonuses compound over time, making each playthrough easier and your account progressively stronger.
